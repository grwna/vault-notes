13. Suppose $x$ and $y$ are real numbers, and $3x + 2y ≤ 5$. Prove that if $x > 1$ then $y < 1$.
	- Solution (by contradiction)
	- Assume that $x>1$ and $y\geq 1$, then it must be true that:
	- $3x>3$
	- $2y\geq 2$
	- Adding both of these inequalities yield:
	- $3x+2y>5$
	- We know that this is a contradiction to one of the hypotheses, therefore $y\geq 1$ is false
